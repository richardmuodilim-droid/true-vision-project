# CLAUDE DESIGN — FULL SESSION PLAYBOOK (from zero)
*Run this as one session, top to bottom. Setup: NEW design · Design system: NONE · strongest model available. Copy each stage when its moment comes.*

---

## STAGE 1 — THE ZERO PROMPT (first message)

You are one of the best streetwear creative directors in the world, the kind who has built cult brands from nothing. I'm giving you almost total freedom. Here is everything you get, and nothing more:

THE BRAND: True Vision Project (TVP). A locked, invitation-only streetwear brand built from nothing by two young founders — one from a small town in Italy, one from a small town in Ireland — both from working families that sacrificed everything quietly. The brand's theme is VISION: seeing what others don't, having a vision for your life when nobody believes it. The website cannot be opened without a secret code — no ads, no public shop, you have to be let in. The people who wear it are the overlooked ones: small-town kids, immigrants, silent builders. Est. 2026.

THE LOGO: a serif "TV" monogram — the T and V interlocked like a ligature, elegant, sharp, almost like an old publishing house mark. This is the only fixed visual asset. Build around it.

THE PRODUCT: the first drop — one heavyweight boxy tee, limited to 50 numbered pieces, around €38. Print must be producible as a 1 or 2 color screen print.

YOUR TASK: Show me FIVE radically different design directions for this first tee. Not five versions of one idea — five different creative universes. Each direction must come from a different territory of design thinking, and none of them can resemble: eye charts, plain slogan tees, mall-kiosk graphics, gothic fonts, flames, or anything currently trending. For each direction give me: a name, the back design (the hero), the front detail, and one sentence on why nobody has done this. At least two directions should be something you believe has genuinely never been made in streetwear.

THE BAR: each concept must make a stranger physically stop someone on the street and ask about it. It must mean something on a second look that it didn't reveal on the first. And a 20-year-old rapper and a 40-year-old design collector should both want it. If a direction wouldn't survive ten years in someone's wardrobe, kill it before showing me.

Work with full conviction. Surprise me. Start.

---

## STAGE 2 — PRESSURE (after the 5 directions arrive)

Now be your own harshest critic. Kill the two weakest directions and tell me exactly why they died. For the three survivors: push each one 30% further — more extreme, more confident, more unforgettable. Show all three pushed versions side by side.

---

## STAGE 3 — COLLISION (pick one direction, replace [X])

Take direction [X] and collide it with something true from our world: two founders in different countries who built this over calls at night · a website nobody can enter · every piece numbered by hand · families who sacrificed in silence. Force one of these truths INTO the design physically — not as words, as a design mechanic. Show me 3 collisions.

---

## STAGE 4 — COMMITMENT (replace [name])

We're choosing [name]. It is now the only design in the world. Develop it completely: the back at full craft, front detail, the flat print-file view, and mocked on a cream heavyweight tee on a dark grainy background. One-color screen print discipline. This version should look ready to sell for €38 tonight.

---

## STAGE 5 — CRAFT PASSES (one line at a time, max 3 rounds)

Use precise pushes, never "make it better":
- "Scale the hero element up 40%."
- "More silence around it — double the empty space."
- "Age the paper, keep the letters crisp."
- "Lower the contrast of the small elements so the hero owns the shirt."
- "More like an official document, less like a poster."
- "Make it 30% more [bold / strange / quiet]."

## VARIETY TRICKS
- Same prompt, fresh chat, second run — compare the two.
- "Show me 3 visual variations of this exact direction."
- Never continue a session past Stage 5 — export and stop.

## THE EXIT RULE
One evening. One winner. Export it (highest resolution) and bring it to Fable — print files, supplier package, and the drop follow same-day. No winner tonight = the Vision Chart goes to print as-is. Either way, Edition 01 exists this week.
