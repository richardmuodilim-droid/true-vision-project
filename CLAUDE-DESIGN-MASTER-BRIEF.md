# PART 1 — THE SOUL (design from this, always)

## The origin story (true, word for word)

Two small towns. Two countries. One road.

One founder grew up in a small town in Italy. One grew up in a small town in Ireland. Different languages, different skies — the same story underneath. Ordinary households. Families that didn't have much but never made you feel it. People who worked hard, asked for little, and gave everything they had. No silver spoons. No shortcuts. No connections.

That kind of background does one of two things: it makes you accept less, or it makes you refuse to. **We refused.** Not out of anger — out of love, for the people who sacrificed quietly, without applause, without ever asking for anything back.

We didn't start True Vision because we had money or because someone believed in us. We started it because we had nothing — and we decided that was enough to begin. We built it for our families. To prove that where you start does not decide where you finish.

## What "True Vision" means

It is not a clothing brand. It is a mindset: the ability to look honestly at where you are — with nothing, with odds stacked against you — and still see clearly where you're going. A vision so certain that no doubt can take it from you. **The theme this brand owns is VISION: seeing what others don't.** Everything you design must serve this theme.

## Who wears it

The overlooked ones. The kids from small towns, the immigrants, the ones working in silence, building without applause, carrying their family's name like a responsibility. When they put on this shirt, they should feel *seen* — like the brand looked at them and said: I know exactly who you are.

## The model (why the design must whisper)

The website (truevisionproject.com) is LOCKED. It cannot be opened without an access code or a member's invite. No ads, no public shop. The brand grows by word of mouth, gifted pieces to rising artists, and people literally asking "what is that, and how do I get in?" The design is the advertising. If it explains itself completely at first glance, it has failed — it must make people come closer.

# PART 2 — THE DESIGN SYSTEM (hard rules, zero exceptions)

- **Colors:** cream #F5F3EE and near-black #111111. That's the entire palette. Deep dark #0a0909 allowed as campaign background only. No third color has ever appeared on this brand and never will. No color accents, no gradients in print artwork (atmospheric darkness in campaign photography is fine).
- **Type voices:** Space Mono, uppercase = the technical/archive voice (chart text, labels, statements, numbers). Cormorant Garamond, often italic = the emotional serif voice (campaign headlines, certificate title — sparingly, it must feel like a rare moment of humanity inside a clinical world). Inter = plain body text only.
- **The aesthetic in one sentence:** official clinical documents from a 1970s institution — eye charts, test results, stamped certificates — that have aged fifty years in a drawer, recomposed with the precision of a luxury house.
- **Recurring motifs (our visual DNA):** thin corner marks (crop-mark style), hairline rules, film grain, numbered fields (N° ___ / 50), bracketed labels in the style [ LIKE THIS ], acuity marks (20/200 → 20/20), the TV monogram (serif ligature mark), stamps and signature lines.
- **Texture philosophy:** age and imperfection in the SUBSTRATE (paper grain, faint ink-press variance, slight plate misregistration), never in the TYPE itself — letters stay crisp and confident. Aged paper, sharp mind.
- **Print reality (garment artwork):** must survive as a ONE-COLOR screen print. No halftones finer than a 55 mesh can hold, no photographic elements, minimum crisp stroke ~0.3mm, minimum type cap-height 3mm.
- **Banned forever:** the words "authentic", "artisan", "craft", "elevated", "bespoke"; the symbol "$" (always "€"); the word "restock"; flames, drips, graffiti, gothic blackletter, Y2K chrome, hype-brand clichés of any kind; Title Case in mono text (always UPPERCASE).
