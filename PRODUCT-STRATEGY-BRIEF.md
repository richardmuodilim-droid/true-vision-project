## STALL STRATEGY (IRELAND + UK)

### Why Stalls?

1. **Direct feedback:** Talk to customers face-to-face
2. **Community building:** People meet the brand, not just the product
3. **Content goldmine:** Every stall = 100+ customer photos for social
4. **Regional exclusives:** Test demand by geography
5. **Viral potential:** Local news, TikTok from stall location

### Target Locations

#### IRELAND
- **Dublin** — City center (Grafton St area)
  - Demographic: Young male 16-30, urban tracksuit culture
  - Footfall: High (shopping district)
  - Exclusive colourway: Purple/Black (TVP-002-PB)

#### UK
- **Manchester** — Northern Quarter or Trafford Centre
  - Demographic: Roadmen, estate culture, streetwear scene
  - Footfall: High
  - Exclusive colourway: Light Blue/Grey (TVP-002-LG)

- **Liverpool** — Bold Street or city center
  - Demographic: Similar to Manchester, strong community
  - Footfall: High
  - Exclusive colourway: Pink/Black (TVP-002-PK)

### Stall Execution

**Timeline:** 1-week stall per location (rotating Aug-Oct 2026)

**Setup:**
- Simple table + branded banner
- Product display (mannequin in tracksuit, folded stock)
- iPad for signups (offline + online)
- Camera (document the experience)

**Activities:**
- Sell tracksuit (€70)
- Take photos of customers in new fits
- Record short video interviews (why TVP?)
- Collect feedback for Drop 003

**Content:**
- Instagram stories from each stall (daily)
- TikTok: "We're in Dublin today" → unbox reactions
- YouTube: Stall vlog (1 per location)

**Metrics:**
- Units sold per location
- Email signups
- Social mentions
- Regional demand signals (feeds Drop 004 planning)

## COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### What TVP Is NOT

- Fast fashion (we make limited runs)
- Hype beast brand (we don't chase trends)
- Lifestyle brand (we're not selling a fantasy)
- Direct-to-consumer commodity (we're not competing on price)

### What TVP IS

- A **documentation of a mission** (the actual statement)
- Built by **real people** from **real places** (Wexford + Bergamo)
- Sold **direct to people who get it** (tracksuit culture, artists, collectors)
- **Scarce by design** (24 units = proof of value)
- **Honest about numbers** ("Drop 001 sold out in 24 hours" is our billboard)

### Competitive Advantage

| Competitor | TVP | Why TVP Wins |
|---|---|---|
| High-street fashion | Generic design, no story | Our story is the product |
| Hype streetwear | Overhyped, produced at scale | We're intentionally scarce |
| Direct-to-consumer | Compete on price | We compete on credibility |
| Luxury brands | Untouchable, gatekeeping | We're accessible but exclusive |

## BRAND GUIDELINES (SHORT VERSION)

### Voice
- Honest about numbers ("24 units in 24 hours")
- Story-first (why it exists)
- Conversational (not corporate)
- Specific places (Wexford, Bergamo)

### Visuals
- Monochrome (black on white/cream)
- Grain overlay (texture, not smooth)
- Corner marks (cinema/document aesthetic)
- Typography hierarchy (serif for emotion, mono for info)

### No-No's
- Don't use: "bespoke," "artisan," "elevated," "authentic," "curated"
- Don't hide the story (numbers = marketing)
- Don't mass-produce (scarcity is the point)
- Don't fake it (everything must be real)
